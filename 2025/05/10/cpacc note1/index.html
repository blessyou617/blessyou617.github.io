📘 CPACC Study Summary – Day 1

✅ What Is a Disability?

  • A disability is a condition that affects a person’s ability to do daily activities.
  • Disability is not just about the person — it often results from barriers in the environment.
  • Example: A person who uses a wheelchair is only “disabled” by stairs, not by the wheelchair itself.

🔠 Key Vocabulary

Term Meaning
Disability A condition that limits abilities / 残疾
Barrier Something that creates difficulty / 障碍
Accessible Easy for everyone to use / 无障碍的
Environment The space or world around us / 环境

♿ Categories of Disabilities (9 Types)

Category Description / Example
Visual Disabilities Blindness, low vision, color blindness (e.g., screen reader)
Auditory Disabilities Deaf or hard of hearing (e.g., captions, sign language)
Deaf-Blindness Both hearing and vision loss (e.g., braille, tactile sign language)
Speech Disabilities Trouble speaking or using speech (e.g., text-to-speech devices)
Mobility & Body Structure Trouble moving or using limbs (e.g., wheelchairs, ramps)
Cognitive Disabilities Affects thinking, memory, learning (e.g., dyslexia, ADHD)
Seizure Disabilities Conditions like epilepsy triggered by lights or patterns
Psychological/Psychiatric Anxiety, depression, PTSD
Multiple/Compound Disabilities A person has more than one disability

🚧 Types of Barriers

Type Examples
Physical Stairs, narrow doorways, no ramps
Digital/ICT No alt text, no keyboard access, low contrast
Communication No captions, no interpreter, complex language
Attitudinal Stereotypes, treating disabled people as helpless
Systemic/Organizational Inflexible rules, inaccessible hiring process

✅ You Answered Correctly:

  • Cognitive Disabilities affect thinking and memory.
  • A communication barrier = A job interview without a sign language interpreter.
